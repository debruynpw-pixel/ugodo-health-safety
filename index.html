Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know

What is occupational health and safety?
OHS is the field concerned with the safety, health and welfare of people at work. In South Africa, the Occupational Health and Safety Act No. 85 of 1993 mandates employers to provide and maintain a safe workplace for all employees.
Is a Health & Safety file legally required?
Yes. Under Construction Regulation 5(1)(b) of the OHS Act, every construction project must have a Health and Safety Plan and File. Without it, your project can be stopped by a DoL inspector — and you may face significant penalties.
What training courses do you offer?
We offer First Aid (Level 1, 2 & 3), Basic Fire Fighting, Working at Heights, Construction Regulations, COIDA (Compensation for Occupational Injuries and Diseases), and Emergency Preparedness. All training can be conducted at your premises.
How long does a workplace H&S audit take?
A standard workplace audit typically takes between half a day and two days, depending on the size and complexity of your operation. We provide a detailed written report with findings and recommended corrective actions.
What areas in KZN do you serve?
We are based in Westville and serve clients throughout KwaZulu-Natal, including Durban, Pinetown, Umhlanga, Ballito, Pietermaritzburg, Richards Bay, and surrounding areas. Contact us for sites outside KZN.
What is COIDA and do I need to comply?
COIDA (Compensation for Occupational Injuries and Diseases Act) covers employees who suffer occupational injuries or diseases. All employers must register with the Compensation Fund and submit annual returns. We assist with full COIDA compliance and claims management.
How do pre-employment medicals work?
A pre-employment medical assesses whether a candidate is medically fit for a specific job role. Tests vary by job type but typically include vision, hearing, lung function, blood pressure, and general health screening. We issue a Certificate of Fitness upon completion.
